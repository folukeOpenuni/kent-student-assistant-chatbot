University of Kent – Key Information

## 1. Admissions

- **Undergraduate applications (UK/Ireland):** Applications are made via UCAS; Kent welcomes applications after the main UCAS deadline and will accept applications for many courses up to 30 June (after which eligible applications go into Clearing). 
  - Main page: https://www.kent.ac.uk/courses/undergraduate/apply

- **International undergraduate applicants:** International students can apply either via UCAS or directly to Kent, but direct applications are only for non‑UK/Irish applicants who pay overseas fees and are not using UCAS at all. 

- **Part‑time applicants:** Part‑time students apply directly to the University via the online form linked from each course page; there is no fixed closing date, but you are advised to apply as early as possible. 

- **Entry requirements:** Kent’s minimum matriculation requirement is typically two A‑levels at grade E or equivalent, but most courses have higher, course‑specific requirements published in the online prospectus and programme specifications.  The general entry‑requirements page also notes you should normally be at least 17 years old by 20 September in the year of entry.
  - Entry requirements overview: https://www.kent.ac.uk/courses/undergraduate/apply/entry-requirements

- **How applications are assessed:** Admissions procedures follow UCAS rules for undergraduates; decisions consider academic record (achieved and predicted grades), motivation, relevant experience, extracurriculars, and school references. 

- **Key applicant hub:** Undergraduate applicants can use the dedicated “Applicants” hub to find info on offer‑holder days, accommodation, funding and preparing for arrival.
  - Applicants hub: https://www.kent.ac.uk/applicants 

- **Admissions contacts:** You can enquire about your application via the KentVision application portal or by contacting the Recruitment and Admissions Office, with contact details provided from the apply pages and via the central “Contact us” page.
  - General contact page: https://www.kent.ac.uk/contact-us


## 2. Assessments

- **Assessment regulations framework:** Kent has a formal Assessment Regulations Framework that sets university‑wide principles on validity, reliability, standards and degree classifications, with detailed guidance on design, conduct and marking of assessments.
  - Overview: https://www.kent.ac.uk/education/quality-assurance/marking-assessment-and-awards

- **Assessment types and workload:** Assessments are grouped into three main types (outlined in Annex 1), and no single assessment component can count for less than 20% or more than 80% of the final module mark, to ensure balanced workload and weighting.
- **Marking and grading scale:** For most modules, marks are recorded out of 100, except where a pass/fail or fail/pass/merit/distinction scale is explicitly used. Kent uses a “categorical marking scale” with fixed percentage points in each classification band (e.g. 100, 95, 85, 82 for First‑class/Distinction) for assessments that require qualitative judgement such as essays, reports, dissertations and exam questions.
- **Moderation and external examiners:** Assessments are normally first‑marked and then moderated (or double‑marked) by academic staff, and external examiners may recommend systematic adjustment of marks only after reviewing evidence and unresolved marker disagreements.
- **Pass marks and reassessment:** For levels 3–6 (undergraduate), the pass mark is 40%; for level 7 modules (taught postgraduate) it is higher (commonly 50%), with reassessment offered either on a like‑for‑like basis or via specified reassessment formats, as detailed in the Guidance for Examiners.

- **Feedback expectations:** University regulations require that assessment and marking criteria are supplied in advance, and that feedback clearly explains strengths, weaknesses, and reasons for the mark, and is returned in time for students to use it before their next assessment on that module.There is also a dedicated Assessment and Feedback Strategy that emphasises alignment with learning outcomes, scaffolding, and research‑informed practice.

## 3. Deadlines

- **UCAS/application deadlines for 2026 entry (typical cycle):**
  - Applications open from September 2025.
  - 15 October: deadline for Kent and Medway Medical School (and Oxbridge/medicine‑type courses generally).
  - 14 January 2026: main UCAS deadline for most undergraduate courses.
  - 30 June: last date to apply via UCAS for most courses before applications are entered into Clearing; Kent states it will generally accept applications up to 30 June where places remain.
  - UCAS Extra opens in late February for applicants with no offers or who have declined all offers.
  - Later dates (e.g. late July, September, October) relate to reply deadlines, final UCAS application date and Clearing close, and are listed in Kent’s application timeline.
  - Application timeline page: https://www.kent.ac.uk/courses/undergraduate/apply/application-timeline

- **Term and closure dates (academic calendar):**
  - The “University term and closure dates” page gives official term dates for undergraduate and postgraduate programmes and notes that academic years typically start in late September or early October.
  - A separate “Term weeks” page shows, week‑by‑week, the 2025/26 calendar with labels for Welcome Week, term weeks, exam/summer term, and vacations (e.g. Autumn term from week starting 6 October 2025, Spring term from week starting 12 January 2026).
  - Term dates: https://student.kent.ac.uk/studies/university-term-and-closure-dates
  - Term weeks: https://student.kent.ac.uk/studies/timetabling/term-weeks

- **Programme‑specific and non‑standard dates:** Some programmes, such as Kent and Medway Medical School (KMMS), have different term patterns, listed separately on the term‑dates page under “KMMS term dates.”


## 4. Wellbeing

- **Student Support and Wellbeing (SSW):** This central service provides holistic support for mental health, disability, autism, specific learning differences (including ADHD and dyslexia), and accessibility, as well as advice on academic adjustments. 
  - Overview: https://www.kent.ac.uk/student-support 
  - MyKent health & wellbeing: https://student.kent.ac.uk/support/wellbeing 

- **Mental health support team:** The Mental Health Team includes Mental Health Advisers and Mentors who can help with stress, anxiety, depression, adjustment to university and relationship issues, and can create Inclusive Learning Plans (ILPs) that set out reasonable adjustments for teaching and assessments.

- **Counselling and specialist wellbeing:** Counselling offers confidential space to work on thoughts, feelings and behaviours, while specialist wellbeing staff support students around sexual assault/harassment, bullying, discrimination and hate incidents, and also provide tailored support for care‑experienced, caregiving and estranged students.

- **24/7 support:** All Kent students have access to 24/7 phone, text and WhatsApp support from trained mental‑health professionals through Kent’s partner Spectrum Life, alongside on‑campus support from the University Nursing Service and Campus Security.

- **Student‑led wellbeing initiatives:** Kent has a Wellbeing Student Advisory Board in partnership with Kent Union, bringing student voices into mental‑health strategy; the BBC has highlighted this as an initiative aimed at making support “meaningful and accessible to everyone.”


## 5. Finance

- **Tuition fee information (general):** Kent maintains a central tuition‑fees portal covering undergraduate, postgraduate and foundation‑year fees, with notes that fees may rise in line with government caps and are subject to parliamentary approval.
  - Tuition fees overview: https://www.kent.ac.uk/tuition-fees

- **Undergraduate tuition fees (Home students):** For 2025/26, government policy has raised the cap for home undergraduates in England to £9,535 per year (subject to final ratification), and Kent notes this applies to both new and returning students. For 2026/27, a further increase to £9,790 has been announced, again subject to approval.

- **Postgraduate and research fees:** Postgraduate taught and research fees vary by course; UKRI‑linked postgraduate research fees for UK students are updated annually, with 2024/25 full‑time reference figures given as around £4,786 (with part‑time about half).

- **Foundation‑year fees:** Separate tables list foundation‑year tuition for 2025/26 by course (e.g. Economics, Finance and Investment), with standard notes that tuition may increase in later years and fee‑status assessment depends on nationality and residence.

- **Scholarships and bursaries (general):**
  - Undergraduate scholarships page: lists scholarships confirmed so far for 2026–27 entry, filterable by School and fee status.
  - The “Scholarships and funding” page for schools/colleges details specific awards such as the Kent UKAT Ambition Scholarship, Kent Access to HE Ambition Scholarship and Kent Ambition to Succeed Scholarship, typically worth £500–£2,000 per stage of undergraduate study for eligible students.
  - Undergraduate scholarships: https://www.kent.ac.uk/scholarships/undergraduate

- **Kent Financial Support Package (KFSP):** This package offers cash bursaries (commonly £1,000 per stage) to eligible full‑time undergraduates, including extra support for four‑year programmes with sandwich or foundation years; the bursary is paid in two instalments throughout the year and does not need to be repaid.

- **Sanctuary Scholarships:** In partnership with the Article 26 project, Kent offers up to five Sanctuary Scholarships a year for undergraduates from forced‑migration backgrounds, including full tuition‑fee waivers and a £2,000 yearly bursary, plus pastoral support and additional small‑grant funding.

- **Further finance information:**
  - Undergraduate fees and funding: https://www.kent.ac.uk/courses/undergraduate/fees-and-funding
  - Postgraduate fees and funding: https://www.kent.ac.uk/courses/postgraduate/fees-and-funding

## 7. Campus Life

- **Canterbury campus life:** Kent’s Canterbury campus has six residential/academic colleges (Darwin, Eliot, Keynes, Rutherford, Turing, Woolf) that form close‑knit communities with social spaces, study areas and support networks; every student belongs to a college. The campus supports over 250 student groups covering academic, cultural, social and special interests, with an emphasis on developing skills and enhancing employability through extracurricular involvement.

- **Medway campus life:** At Medway, Kent is part of the “Universities at Medway” partnership with Greenwich and Canterbury Christ Church, sharing social spaces and facilities across institutions. Students can join societies, enjoy shared social areas, and access industry‑standard equipment to smooth the transition into the workplace.

- **Living in university accommodation:** The “Living in university accommodation” pages describe facilities on the Canterbury campus (and Pier Quays at Medway) including furnished rooms, inclusive utility bills, internet, contents insurance, plus social/study hubs and everyday support from Colleges and Community Life Teams. The same pages emphasise campus life as “much more than studying”, highlighting events, support services, sports and community activities.

- **Sports, societies and social spaces:** University and Kent Union channels promote over 250 societies and a large sports offer, with dedicated times like Wednesday afternoons for sport (no lectures) and venues such as campus cafés, students’ union spaces and The Venue nightclub.

- **Support embedded in campus life:** Campus‑based support includes careers and employability services, health services, housekeeping, college‑based pastoral teams and the award‑winning Student Support and Wellbeing team, all integrated into residential and social settings.

- **More on campus life:**
  - Canterbury campus life: https://www.kent.ac.uk/locations/canterbury/campus-life
  - Medway campus life: https://www.kent.ac.uk/locations/medway/campus-life


## 8. General Enquiries

- **Central University contact:**
  - “Contact us” hub with phone numbers, email addresses and enquiry forms for general enquiries, course information, media, alumni, and more: https://www.kent.ac.uk/contact-us

- **Student‑facing enquiry hubs:**
  - **Nexus (Templeman Library):** One‑stop front desk for student queries (fees, registration, ID cards, general admin, signposting to specialist teams); open hours and details are on the Library opening‑hours and support page.
  - **Arrivals Hub:** Operates at key intake periods to handle in‑person queries about student ID cards, fee payments, and visa checks for international students; when closed, students are directed to Nexus.

- **Students’ Union helpdesk:** Kent Union’s helpdesk uses a ticketing system to manage student support and advice queries for matters such as housing, money, academic issues and complaints, complementing University services.

- **Online portals:**
  - **MyKent** (student portal): main gateway to study information, support services, term dates and digital services (linked across multiple pages).
  - **TARGETconnect:** system used for careers and employability bookings and resources.
